Order of Work

1. Strip cement render
Work in sections, starting below ground floor window. SDS chisel + hand tools near windows.
2. Clean stone faces
Wire brush and moderate pressure wash (1000–1500 PSI, fan nozzle). Remove all cement residue.
3. Remove bitumen at base
Strip the black coating at ground level — it's sealing moisture in.
4. Dig out tarmac & trench for French drain
Strip tarmac 500–600mm from wall. Trench 300mm+ deep, below internal floor level.
5. Install French drain
Geotextile on outer edge/base. Square guttering at bottom with fall to downhill side. Clean 20mm gravel fill.
6. Rake out old mortar
Remove loose/crumbly mortar to depth of 2× joint width. Leave sound lime mortar in place.
7. Repoint with lime (top down)
NHL 3.5 mortar, 1:2.5–1:3 mix. Damp wall first. Pack firmly. Brush back when thumbprint firm. Cover and mist for 3–5 days.
8. Limewash
Lime putty thinned. 3–5 thin coats on dampened wall. Allow each coat to dry between applications.

French Drain — Key Principles

  • NO membrane on the wall — wall must breathe
  • Gravel must be clean, washed 20mm — no fines
  • Fall is critical — water must go somewhere
  • Discharge runs downhill to soakaway or drain
  • Corner junction: mitre gutters or use angle piece

Mortar Specification

  • NHL 3.5 hydraulic lime
  • Ratio: 1:2.5 to 1:3 (lime:sand)
  • Sharp, well-graded, washed sand
  • Can add stone dust for colour match
  • Mix thoroughly, let stand before use
  • Working time: several hours (not like cement)

Conditions

  • NOT in direct hot sun
  • NOT below 5°C or risk of frost
  • NOT in heavy rain
  • Best months: May–June or Sept–Oct
  • Damp the wall before pointing
  • Keep damp for 3–5 days after

Materials & Mix Ratios

Pointing Mortar

  • Lime: NHL 3.5 (Secil, St Astier, or Singleton Birch)
  • Sand: Sharp, well-graded, washed, coarse
  • Ratio: 1 part lime : 2.5–3 parts sand
  • Quantity: 4–5 × 25kg bags lime for front elevation
  • Optional: Stone dust or coarse grit for colour match

Limewash

  • Base: Lime putty (NOT NHL powder)
  • Mix: Thin with water to milk-like consistency
  • Coats: 3–5 thin coats minimum
  • Tint: Natural pigments if desired (historically off-white/cream)
  • Application: Dampen wall, apply thin, let dry between coats

French Drain Materials

  • Square plastic guttering (4m+)
  • Joints, end cap, square-to-round adapter
  • Geotextile membrane
  • Clean 20mm washed gravel (2 bulk bags)
  • Solid pipe for discharge run

Safety

Always Wear

  • FFP3 mask — silica dust causes silicosis. Not optional.
  • Sealed goggles — cement chips are alkaline, cause chemical burns
  • Ear defenders — SDS drill for hours damages hearing
  • Heavy gloves — sharp cement and stone edges

Structural Warning Signs — STOP work if you find:

  • Wall bulging outward 25mm+ over 1m run
  • Diagonal cracking through stonework (not just render)
  • Cracked or dropped lintels above openings
  • Wall feels hollow when tapped / stones move when pushed
  • New cracks appearing on internal walls while stripping

Services

  • Check where electric supply enters building
  • Check gas meter location
  • Check phone/broadband cable entry
  • Use cable detector on wall before chiselling
  • Hit a live cable with SDS chisel = serious injury

Other Checks

  • Consider asbestos test on render (£20–30 lab test)
  • Check listed building / conservation area status
  • Pavement licence may be needed if debris could fall on public path
  • Cover windows with plywood/cardboard when chiselling nearby

Tips & Lessons Learned

Render Stripping

  • Start from cracks — SDS chisel gets under the edge easily
  • Render peels away from under lintels — natural weak point
  • Work in sections, finish on natural break lines not straight edges
  • 40mm flat chisel does 80% of the work
  • Use pry bar to lever off large sections once edges are loosened
  • Keep render rubble — usable as patio sub-base fill

Lime vs Cement — Key Differences

  • Lime is stickier/more putty-like than cement
  • Much longer working time — hours, not minutes
  • Must stay damp to cure (opposite of cement)
  • Softer than stone — sacrificial layer, protects the stone
  • Flexes with building movement instead of cracking
  • Can't add water if it stiffens — rework by beating

Stone Condition

  • Cracked stones: pack lime mortar into cracks, limewash over
  • Spalled faces: limewash will consolidate, or turn stone around
  • Drill holes from old fixings: fill with mortar
  • Brick packing pieces: leave in place, they're doing their job
  • Loose small stones: bed back in with lime mortar when repointing

Historical Reference

  • 1890s photo shows cottage with limewash — fairly opaque white
  • 1950s photo shows bare/limewashed stone, pre-cement render
  • Cement render likely applied 1960s–70s
  • Multiple neighbouring properties have exposed stone — confirms it suits the stone
  • Restoration puts cottage back to original appearance

Order of Work

1. Survey & planning
Damp survey, assess chimney breast, check for removal, plan layout and finishes.
2. Strip plaster from walls
Test for asbestos before stripping. Remove all existing plaster back to masonry.
3. Open fireplace
Sweep chimney, assess flue condition, remove blocking board or brick infill, assess lintel, form hearth.
4. Structural checks
Confirm lintel integrity over fireplace opening. Check any walls being altered for load-bearing status.
5. First fix & prep
Electrics first fix, treat any damp, let walls dry before replastering.
6. Replaster
Lime plaster to match breathable wall system. Two coat work: scratch coat + finish coat.
7. Joinery & fireplace surround
Fit architrave, skirting, window boards. Install fireplace surround and hearth.
8. Decorate
Allow lime plaster to fully carbonate (min 4 weeks) before painting.

Safety

Always Wear When Stripping

  • FFP3 mask — old plaster dust can contain silica and other hazards
  • Sealed goggles — plaster debris
  • Ear defenders — SDS work
  • Heavy gloves — sharp edges, lime burns from old plaster

Asbestos — Essential Check

  • Artex (textured coatings) pre-2000 may contain chrysotile asbestos
  • Some older plasters and adhesives also contain it
  • Send sample to accredited lab before disturbing — £20–40
  • If positive, use licensed contractor for removal
  • Do NOT dry-sand or mechanically strip if asbestos suspected

Hidden Services

  • Use cable/pipe detector before any SDS work on walls
  • Isolate electrics to the room at consumer unit before stripping
  • Mark known cable routes before starting
  • Gas pipes in walls are rare but possible in older properties

Structural

  • Do not remove or alter any wall without confirming it is non-load-bearing
  • Chimney breast removal above requires steel support — consult engineer
  • STOP if you see diagonal cracking through masonry, not just plaster
  • Get structural engineer sign-off on lintel before opening fireplace if in doubt
